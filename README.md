#  Environmental Noise Assessment

## Overview
This project focuses on Environmental Noise Assessment and Impact Analysis conducted at the AB-1 Construction Zone of Delhi Technological University (DTU) as part of an EIA Survey Camp.

The study evaluates environmental noise levels, identifies critical noise hotspots, compares observed data with CPCB Silence Zone standards, and proposes mitigation measures for regulatory compliance.

---

## Objectives
- Assess noise pollution levels around the construction zone
- Compare observed noise levels with CPCB standards
- Identify high-noise impact zones
- Analyze impacts on students, laboratories, and surrounding areas
- Recommend mitigation and environmental management measures

---

## Tools & Technologies
- Sound Level Meter (Cesva SC260)
- QGIS (Noise Contour Mapping)
- Environmental Noise Monitoring Techniques
- Data Analysis & Visualization

---

## Study Highlights
- Conducted monitoring at 5 different locations around DTU
- Measured parameters including:
  - Leq
  - Lmax
  - Lmin
  - L10
  - L90
- Generated Noise Contour Maps using IDW Interpolation in QGIS
- Compared results with CPCB Silence Zone standards

---

## Key Findings
- All monitored locations exceeded the CPCB daytime limit of 50 dBA
- Department of Science Building recorded the highest noise level (72.53 dBA)
- Maximum observed peak reached 90.2 dBA
- Construction activities and traffic were major noise contributors
- Several locations showed continuous acoustic stress without effective quiet periods

---

## Mitigation Measures Suggested
- Acoustic noise barriers
- Soundproof enclosures for equipment
- Low-noise construction technology
- Strategic scheduling of high-noise activities
- Real-time noise monitoring systems
